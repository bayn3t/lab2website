The Legacy of Oasis

Oasis's legacy is defined by their role as leaders of the Britpop movement in the 1990s, characterized by their anthemic rock music, massive stadium shows like the Knebworth concerts, and the intense but commercially successful rivalry between brothers Noel and Liam Gallagher. Despite internal conflicts leading to their 2009 breakup, their music sold over 75 million records and influenced many subsequent bands. They are celebrated for iconic albums like Definitely Maybe and (What's the Story) Morning Glory? and their lasting impact on British culture.
